Normal beat

normal resting heart rate for adults ranges from 60 to 100 beats per minute.

Supraventricular premature beats

Supraventricular premature beats are atrial contractions triggered by ectopic foci rather than the sinoatrial node. They arise within the atria (atrial premature beats) or, through retrograde conduction, in the atrioventricular node

Premature ventricular contractions (PVCs)

are extra heartbeats that begin in one of the heart's two lower pumping chambers (ventricles). These extra beats disrupt the regular heart rhythm, sometimes causing a sensation of a fluttering or a skipped beat in the chest.

Fusion of ventricular and normal beat

occurs when a supraventricular and a ventricular impulse coincide to produce a hybrid complex.
