Normal beat

The normal beat of the heart is a rhythmic contraction and relaxation of the heart muscle, which is responsible for pumping blood throughout the body. The normal heart rate for an adult at rest is typically between 60 and 100 beats per minute.

Supraventricular premature beats

Supraventricular premature beat is a heartbeat that occurs earlier than expected and originates from the upper chambers of the heart, known as the atria. This is why it's called "supraventricular," meaning above the ventricles. These premature beats can occur in anyone and may not necessarily indicate a serious health issue.

Premature ventricular contractions (PVCs)

Premature ventricular contraction" (PVC) is a type of abnormal heart rhythm in which the heart's lower chambers, called the ventricles, beat too early. This can lead to an irregular heartbeat and may be felt as a skipped beat or palpitations.

Fusion of ventricular and normal beat

The fusion of ventricular and normal beats is a type of cardiac arrhythmia that occurs when a normal heartbeat and a premature ventricular contraction(PVC) occur at the same time. This can cause a mixture of the normal and abnormal heartbeat, resulting in a unique pattern on an electrocardiogram (ECG).

Unclassifiable beat

An unclassifiable beat refers to a heartbeat that cannot be classified as a normal beat or any specific type of cardiac arrhythmia. This can occur when the electrical activity of the heart does not follow a regular pattern, making it difficult to determine the underlying cause. Unclassifiable beats can be identified through an electrocardiogram (ECG), which records the heart's electrical activity. The ECG may show a unique or irregular pattern that cannot be reliably classified as a normal or abnormal heartbeat.

Myocardial Infarction

A myocardial infarction (MI) occurs when the blood supply to the heart muscle is blocked, usually by a blood clot in one of the coronary arteries. This can cause damage to the heart muscle and can be life-threatening if not treated promptly. Symptoms of a myocardial infarction may include chest pain or discomfort, shortness of breath, nausea or vomiting, lightheadedness or fainting, and pain or discomfort in the arms, jaw, neck, or back.

ECG Classification

NeuralBeats

NeuralBeats is a web application that utilizes convolutional neural networks (CNN) to assist doctors in the classification of ECG images. With NeuralBeats, doctors can quickly and accurately identify the presence of abnormal heart rhythms, providing them with valuable insights to aid in patient diagnosis and treatment.
